## 1. What is an EAD

An Employment Authorization Document (EAD or "work permit") is a card issued by
USCIS that authorizes you to work in the U.S. for a specific period. Not everyone
needs one — some visa holders (H-1B, L-1, O-1) have work authorization tied to
their visa and employer. The EAD provides open-market work authorization (you can
work for any employer).

## 2. Common EAD Categories

EAD applications use category codes on Form I-765. The most common:

| Code | Category | Who |
|---|---|---|
| (a)(12) | TPS holder | Apply with or after I-821 |
| (c)(8) | Asylum applicant (pending) | Must wait 150 days after filing I-589 (if no delays caused by applicant) |
| (c)(9) | Adjustment of status applicant | Pending I-485 |
| (c)(10) | Withholding of removal applicant | Pending withholding case |
| (c)(11) | Parolee | Humanitarian parole, U4U, etc. |
| (c)(26) | H-4 dependent spouse | Only if H-1B spouse has approved I-140 or is in 7th+ year |
| (c)(35)/(c)(36) | CNMI/certain parolees | Specific programs |
| (a)(10) | Withholding granted | Already granted withholding |
| (c)(3)(A)(i) | F-1 post-completion OPT | 12-month initial OPT |
| (c)(3)(C) | F-1 STEM OPT extension | 24-month extension for STEM degrees |
| (c)(33) | DACA recipient | Deferred Action for Childhood Arrivals |
| (c)(14) | Deferred action (non-DACA) | Other deferred action grants |
| (c)(12) | Spouse of L-1 (L-2) | Open-market work authorization |

### Important: know your category code
Your EAD category determines your eligibility, renewal rules, and automatic extension
availability. Using the wrong code delays or denies your application.

> ⚠️ The rules for each EAD category differ — automatic extension eligibility,
> renewal procedures, and validity periods vary by basis. Always verify the
> current rules for the specific category from official USCIS sources.

## 3. How to Apply (Form I-765)

### Filing basics
- **Form:** I-765, Application for Employment Authorization
- **Filing fee:** Varies by category — some are fee-exempt (e.g., asylum-based EADs).
  ⚠️ Check **current** fee at uscis.gov — do not rely on this file for the amount.
- **Where to file:** USCIS lockbox or online (myUSCIS) — depends on your category.
  Check the I-765 instructions for your specific filing address.
- **Online filing:** Available for most categories through your myUSCIS account.

### Required documents (varies by category, but generally)
- Completed Form I-765
- Two passport-style photos
- Copy of current immigration document (I-94, visa, prior EAD)
- Copy of any pending application receipt (I-589, I-485, I-821)
- Government-issued ID
- Filing fee or fee waiver request (I-912)

### Processing times
- Vary significantly by category and service center — 2 to 10+ months
- ⚠️ Check **current** processing times at egov.uscis.gov/processing-times
- **Expedite requests:** You can request expedited processing if you face severe
  financial loss, emergency, or other qualifying hardship. File online or call
  the USCIS Contact Center.

## 4. Automatic Extensions

> ⚠️ Automatic extension rules change by regulation. Always verify which
> categories currently qualify and the extension duration at uscis.gov.

### When your EAD renews automatically
In some categories, filing a timely renewal extends your expiring EAD automatically
while USCIS processes the renewal. The extension period and eligible categories
change over time — always verify at uscis.gov before relying on auto-extension.

### How it works
- You must file Form I-765 renewal before your current EAD expires
- Your current EAD is automatically extended while USCIS processes the renewal
- Carry BOTH your expired EAD card AND the I-797C receipt notice as proof of
  the automatic extension
- Show both documents to your employer for I-9 reverification

### Categories eligible for automatic extension
⚠️ The list of eligible categories has changed over time and continues to change.
Common categories have included (c)(9), (c)(10), (a)(12), (c)(8), and others.
Verify at uscis.gov which categories currently qualify before relying on this.

### Category-specific extension rules
Different EAD categories have different extension mechanics:

- **TPS (a)(12):** Federal Register notices may automatically extend TPS EADs.
  Multiple extension paths may exist for the same person — check country-specific
  FRN notices and USCIS I-9 Central for current extension dates.
- **AOS pending (c)(9):** Typically eligible for 180-day auto-extension on renewal.
- **Asylum pending (c)(8):** Check current eligible category list at uscis.gov.
- **H-4 (c)(26):** Extension tied to underlying H-1B status. H-4 EAD rule has
  been subject to litigation — verify current status.
- **DACA (c)(33):** Extension depends on DACA program status and renewal timing.
- **OPT (c)(3)(B):** STEM OPT 24-month extension has specific employer and
  reporting requirements.

## 5. Combo Card (EAD + Advance Parole)

### What it is
A single card that combines both employment authorization (EAD) and travel permission
(advance parole). Issued to I-485 adjustment applicants who request both.

### How to get one
- When you file I-485, also file I-765 (EAD) and I-131 (Advance Parole)
- USCIS may issue a single combo card instead of separate documents
- Allows you to work AND travel while your green card is pending

### Critical travel warning
- Do NOT travel outside the U.S. without a valid advance parole or combo card
  while your I-485 is pending — leaving without it is considered abandonment of
  your adjustment application (with some exceptions for H-1B/L-1 holders).
- If your combo card has expired and you haven't received a new one, do NOT travel.
  Request expedited processing or wait for the new card.

## 6. Common Issues

### EAD delays and gap in work authorization
- If your EAD renewal is delayed and the auto-extension period expires, you may
  have a gap in work authorization. You cannot legally work during this gap.
- Notify your employer. They must stop your employment until you have valid
  authorization again.
- Consider requesting an expedite with USCIS.
- Contact your congressional representative's office for help — they can make
  inquiries to USCIS on your behalf.

### Employer issues
- Your employer verifies your work authorization through the I-9 process.
- They cannot demand specific documents — you choose which documents to present
  from the acceptable document list (Lists A, B, and C on the I-9 form).
- If your employer refuses to accept valid documents or demands your EAD specifically,
  that may be a violation of anti-discrimination law. Contact the Immigrant and
  Employee Rights Section (IER) at the Department of Justice.

### Lost or stolen EAD
- File Form I-765 as a replacement application (check "replacement" on the form).
- Filing fee applies.
- If you need expedited replacement, request an expedite.

### Name change
- If your name has changed (marriage, court order), file a replacement EAD with
  evidence of the name change (marriage certificate, court order).

### Working without an EAD
- Working without authorization is a serious immigration violation.
- It can bar you from future benefits (adjustment of status, certain visa types).
- If you're unsure about your work authorization, verify before starting work.
